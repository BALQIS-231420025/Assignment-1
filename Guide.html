How to Be A Photographer

 

  1. Complete a diploma/degree/course related to photography, digital or multi media related course.

    • Although employers/clients sometimes value work experiences and skills, having an official certification related to career can give a solid background to build rapport on.
    • A course can also provide variety of skills to learn.
    • It also can help finding one's niche.

  2. Get an internship or entry-level role

    • Entry-level role such as assistant photographer can be the my first exposure to the world of professional photography.
    • Assisting a senior photographer also means we can learn from the seniors and take note about works they do and how they manage the works.
    • For example, scheduling shoots, editing photos, and setting up lighting, backgrounds and equipment before the shoot

  3. Build portfolio

    • Photography portfolio allows you to display your best work, demonstrating your skills and unique style to potential clients and employers.
    • Portfolio is often the first interaction potential clients have with your work. It's crucial that it leaves a positive and lasting impression
    • A comprehensive portfolio establishes your credibility as a professional photographer. It shows that you have experience and a body of work to back up your claims

  4. Networking

    • Networking is the process of building connections and relationships with others, particularly in a professional or career context
    • Relationships drive the creative industry because it build communities, learns from peers, and finding collaboration opportunities.
    • Social media and online platforms are invaluable for showcasing your work and connecting with others

  5. Broaden your career options

    • It also worth to learn other skills related to photography such as videography, video editing and graphic design.
    • Some employers prefer candidates with a wider range of skills.
